Orchestra: The performance is held in a large hall, with wind instruments, string instruments, and percussion instruments all played in harmony. Performers who play different instruments show a large-scale performance, working together under the direction of the conductor. In orchestras, breathing between performers is very important. Not only should the performer focus on the sound of his or her instruments, but also the sounds of other instruments, and play well as the conductor leads.

Chamber music: It consists of three to four performers and usually plays in small halls. Chamber music must be played without a conductor, so breathing between performers is very important. For this reason, the performers who are in sync usually get together and play.
